### Three access layers. One system. Everyone sees exactly what they need to.

Most platforms give you one level of access and ask you to work around it. SolarOps was built from the ground up with layered permissions, so every role operates inside the right boundaries without friction.

**Admin**
Full visibility across every module, every team member, every financial record. Admins see the entire operation, configure access, and maintain control over what each layer of the business can do.

**Staff**
Team members access the modules relevant to their role, nothing more. A sales rep sees the pipeline. A dispatcher sees work orders and alerts. A COO sees performance across teams. Permissions are set by admins and enforced automatically. No manual gatekeeping, no accidental exposure.

**Contractors**
A completely separate portal. Contractors onboard, receive assigned jobs, submit field reports, upload photos, and send invoices, all inside their own environment. They see what they need for their work and nothing about the rest of your operation. No shared logins. No workarounds. Clean separation by design.

### Security that matches the access structure

Client data and business financials require the same discipline as the permissions that protect them. SolarOps is built on that standard.

- Every connection is encrypted in transit. Data is encrypted at rest. Nothing travels or rests in plain text.
- Authentication is managed by enterprise-grade infrastructure. Passwords are hashed. Sessions use cryptographic tokens. No plaintext credentials anywhere in the system.
- The database enforces access at the row level. Even if an access layer were somehow compromised, the data belonging to other users or roles remains unreachable.
- API keys and service credentials live exclusively on the server. They do not appear in browser code, URLs, or client-side storage.
- The contractor portal is architecturally isolated. Contractors cannot reach internal staff data, financial records, or customer details outside their assigned scope.
- Rate limiting, HTML sanitization, and input validation are enforced across all endpoints. The system is hardened against the most common attack vectors.

The result is a platform you can trust with client data, confidently demonstrate to enterprise buyers, and hand to a compliance review without preparation.
