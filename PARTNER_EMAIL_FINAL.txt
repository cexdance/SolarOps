── WHAT SOLAROPS DOES ──────────────────────────────────────────

Let me walk you through what's actually inside it, because I think the scope might surprise you.

SALES & LEAD MANAGEMENT
The platform has a full sales pipeline with a Lead Lobby for incoming prospects, interaction tracking for every call, email, text, note, and meeting, and a gamified leaderboard system that keeps the sales team motivated and accountable. Reps earn XP and advance levels as they close leads. This replaces tools that typically cost $50 to $150 per user per month.

CUSTOMER MANAGEMENT — 360° VIEW
Every customer has a complete profile. You can see their entire history: every work order, every interaction, every invoice, their SolarEdge site ID, PowerCare case numbers, parts tracking, and a full activity log. Everything that has ever happened with that client is in one place.

WORK ORDER MANAGEMENT
Work orders move through a complete lifecycle — from draft, to quote sent, quote approved, scheduled, in progress, completed, invoiced, and paid. Each work order tracks labor costs, parts costs, contractor fees, customer revenue, and profit margin. Quotes are generated directly inside the platform.

CONTRACTOR PORTAL
Contractors have their own secure login. They see their assigned jobs, submit completion reports, upload organized site photos (before, after, serial numbers, voltage tests, inverter photos), and submit invoices. Admins review and approve from the main dashboard. No more back-and-forth over WhatsApp or email.

SOLAREDGE MONITORING
The platform connects to the SolarEdge API and pulls in site alerts automatically. Each alert has a severity level and can be acknowledged, resolved, or linked directly to a work order. This eliminates manual checking of the SolarEdge portal entirely.

BILLING & INVOICING
Invoices are created inside the platform and sync with Xero. Payment tracking, overdue flags, profitability per client, and revenue reports are all built in.

INVENTORY & OPERATIONS
Equipment, tools, and provider management in one place. The Ops Center dashboard gives a real-time view of today's schedule, unbilled jobs, work order status, team activity, and notifications — all visible the moment you log in.

MULTI-USER ROLES & MOBILE
The platform supports Admin, COO, Technician, Sales, and Support roles with appropriate access controls. It works on desktop, tablet, and mobile.

You can see it live right now at: https://solarflow-dashboard-sooty.vercel.app

── WHAT ABOUT THE PANAMA SUITE? ────────────────────────────────

I know our Panama chapter has a CRM suite available at suite.conexsol.com that could potentially be acquired at low or no cost. I want to be fair about this comparison because I think it matters.

The Panama suite is a working Firebase-based platform. It handles some of what we need. I respect that it exists and that the team there built it. But here is what I'd ask you to honestly consider:

It was not built for our workflow. Our operations are specific — SolarEdge integration, optimizer changes, contractor portals, RMA tracking, PowerCare cases, Xero billing. Those are not standard features in a generic suite. Getting the Panama platform to do what SolarOps already does would require significant customization work, and that work would depend on their team's availability and priorities.

It was also built in Firebase with a stack that may or may not be maintainable long-term by someone on our team. SolarOps is built on React and TypeScript — modern, well-documented, and something I can update the same day a change is needed.

And then there is the migration. Moving our customer data, work order history, and contacts into a new system is not a weekend task. It requires planning, mapping, testing, and staff time. Even if the software is free, the implementation is not. A realistic estimate for that migration — in staff time alone — is $3,000 to $6,000.

SolarOps is already built. It already works. It is already holding our data structure. The only question is whether we formalize the arrangement around it.
